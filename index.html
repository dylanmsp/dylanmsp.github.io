Vulnerabilities are common. Vulnerabilities are exploitable weaknesses in systems or services. Programmers aim for perfect code, but each 1,000 lines of code contains up to 20 bugs of varying severity. The exploitation of these bugs by criminals lead to economic or other losses by users and companies. Vulnerabilities are unavoidable and must be mitigated as quickly as possible, using all available resources. Countermeasures play a large role in the exploitability of a vulnerability.

The size of a business is often less important to an attacker than the potential of a business. The data it owns, the personally-identifiable information (PII)

it contains, the opacity of the network for sneaking around, and other factors can be far more desirable than targeting a high-profile company. Most attack groups focus on intelligence gathering. Company employees often unwittingly cause security issues using an open internet connection. 1 in 13 web requests out of every billion leads to malware.
